## On self care

Not to be dramatic but sometimes existing in this world is hard. Like, the idea of getting out of bed and sitting at my desk for several hours a day, or cooking a meal for myself and then washing all the dishes, or even just taking a shower and washing my face‚Äîsometimes it‚Äôs just too much. Sometimes it feels like the best I can do is lay on the couch in yesterday‚Äôs t-shirt and eat a bowl of cereal and play video games. And that‚Äôs cool! I am trying to be a lot more forgiving with myself on those days. But I am also trying to take better care of myself, to at least do the bare minimum. Yes, sure, sheet masks and bubble baths are self care. But so is brushing your teeth and taking a big gulp of water on the hard days. So is cleaning off a tiny area of your desk so you feel a little less stressed while you‚Äôre working. For a while I thought I "wasn't into skincare," because so much of what I saw online was about glamorous ritual and this indulgence into complicated process. It was also largely focused on the face. It took literally re-arranging the phrase‚Äîinstead of self care, it's *taking care of yourself‚Äî*for me to realize self care isn't a trendy thing I'm behind on. The extra, the sometimes superfluous‚Äîan extra special meal, staying in bed longer than normal‚Äîthese things can be folded into taking care of myself, too, but I can't ignore the basics.
